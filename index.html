🗽 Ideas for Exploring NYC

These are ideas, not fixed plans.

Governors Island

Ferry from Lower Manhattan, car-free, bike rentals (the family quad bikes are a hit), giant slide at The Hills.

Official WebsiteMap

The High Line + Little Island + Chelsea Market

Enter the High Line at Gansevoort (south end) or 34th Street and walk it end to end. Little Island, the floating mushroom park on the Hudson, connects right at 14th Street and is free. Chelsea Market is great for lunch, vendors, weird shops, Los Tacos No. 1. You can do all three as one connected afternoon.

High LineLittle IslandChelsea MarketLos Tacos No. 1

Dumbo + Brooklyn Bridge Park

Walk the Brooklyn Bridge from Manhattan into Dumbo (one way only), pizza at Juliana's, Jane's Carousel, Pier 6 playgrounds, Pier 2 roller rink.

Brooklyn Bridge ParkJuliana'sJane's CarouselDUMBO Map

The Tenement Museum

Real preserved immigrant apartments on the Lower East Side. Storytelling-based tours, kids 8-14 are the sweet spot for it.

Official WebsiteMap

The Met rooftop + Central Park

Citi Bikes through the park, Egyptian wing + rooftop installation at the Met. Don't try to do the whole museum.

The MetCentral ParkCiti Bike

Coney Island

Q train subway down. Cyclone, Nathan's, Luna Park, the boardwalk, the sideshow. Feels like a different city. (about an hour from city)

Luna ParkNathan'sMap

SUMMIT One Vanderbilt

Mirror rooms, glass ledges, exterior glass elevator. Best high-up experience in the city for kids.

Official WebsiteMap
